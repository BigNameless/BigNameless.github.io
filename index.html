Over-processed foods have become so common in everyday life that people no longer pay attention to what they eat.

It is essential to know what you eat. Some processed foods often contain excessive amounts of unhealthy additives, such as artificial colorings, flavorings and preservatives, which can alter the metabolism and cause effects.

Poorly processed or over processed foods are a direct risk to your health. Your body is not made for it. Ignoring this is playing with fire. Think of the consequences: from simple food poisoning to serious chronic illness.

Companies pollute to maximize profits, ignoring the long-term damage. Pesticides and herbicides poison the land, water and our food, directly affecting our health and ecosystem.

Food is important for health, but overeating excessively processed foods that do not provide the nutrients the body needs will cause problems in the long term, such as:

  • Obesity Epidemic: Hyper-palatable foods disrupt natural satiety signals leading to overconsumption
  • Cardiovascular Disease: Trans fats and excess sodium damage arteries and increase blood pressure
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Refined carbohydrates cause insulin resistance and blood sugar spikes
  • Gut Health: Lack of fiber destroys microbiome diversity and promotes inflammation
  • Mental Health: Linked to increased rates of depression, anxiety, and ADHD

Being attentive to what we eat will give us a long and healthy life, but do not forget to exercise as a complement.

Food is important for health, eating the right proportions and the nutrients you need will help you to have a better health.

  • Weight Management: Natural foods promote healthy metabolism and satiety
  • Heart Health: Rich in healthy fats, fiber and antioxidants that protect cardiovascular system
  • Blood Sugar Control: Complex carbohydrates and fiber prevent insulin spikes
  • Gut Microbiome: Prebiotic fibers feed beneficial gut bacteria
  • Brain Function: Essential fatty acids and nutrients support cognitive health
  • Longevity: Associated with longer lifespan and reduced chronic disease risk

Being attentive to what we eat will give us a long and healthy life, but do not forget to exercise as a complement.
